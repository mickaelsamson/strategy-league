Moonfall Settlers Rules

Goal. Be the first clan to reach the selected victory score. Settlements are worth 1 point, cities 2 points, Victory Relic cards 1 point, Longest Road 2 points, and Largest Army 2 points.

Setup. Each clan places one settlement and one road in player order, then again in reverse order. The second settlement grants one starting resource from each adjacent non-crater tile.

Turn flow. Roll two dice, collect resources from matching numbered tiles, then build, trade, buy or play one development card, and end the turn.

Resources. Forest gives Wood, Brick gives Brick, Wheat gives Wheat, Pasture gives Sheep, and Mountain gives Ore. A settlement collects 1 resource; a city collects 2.

Building. Roads cost 1 Wood and 1 Brick. Settlements cost 1 Wood, 1 Brick, 1 Wheat, and 1 Sheep. Cities cost 2 Wheat and 3 Ore. Development cards cost 1 Wheat, 1 Sheep, and 1 Ore.

Placement. Settlements must be at least two edges away from every other settlement or city. Outside setup, new settlements must connect to one of your roads. Roads must connect to your road network or one of your buildings.

Oni / seven. On a 7, clans with more than 7 resources discard half at random. The active clan moves the Oni to another tile and steals one random resource from an adjacent rival with resources. The Oni blocks production on its tile.

Development cards. Knight moves the Oni and counts toward Largest Army. Road Building places two free roads. Year of Plenty gives any two resources. Monopoly takes one resource type from every other clan. Victory Relics score points while held.

Trade. Bank trades are normally 4:1, improved to 3:1 by generic ports or 2:1 by matching resource ports. Clan trades require the partner to accept the proposed exchange.

Awards. Longest Road requires at least 5 connected roads and moves only when another clan exceeds the holder. Largest Army requires at least 3 Knights and moves only when another clan exceeds the holder.

Timer and surrender. When the timer is enabled, a human who times out has the dice rolled automatically when needed, then the turn is passed. After 3 timed-out turns, that player surrenders. If at least 2 human clans remain, the surrendered clan is controlled by AI; if only 1 human clan remains, that human wins immediately.
